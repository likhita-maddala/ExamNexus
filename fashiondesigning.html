    • Careers

    • Fashion Designer:

      • Create and design clothing and accessories, staying up-to-date with fashion trends and market demands.

    • Apparel Merchandiser:

      • Coordinate the production and sale of clothing, working with designers, manufacturers, and retailers.

    • Fashion Illustrator:

      • Translate design ideas into visual representations through sketches and illustrations.

    • Pattern Maker:

      • Create templates for cutting fabric pieces to be assembled into garments, ensuring proper fit and design.

    • Fashion Stylist:

      • Coordinate and curate outfits for individuals, models, or events, considering current fashion trends and personal styles.

    • Textile Designer:

      • Create patterns and designs for fabrics, considering texture, color, and manufacturing processes.

    • Accessory Designer:

      • Create and design accessories such as jewelry, handbags, and footwear to complement fashion collections.

    • Fashion Buyer:

      • Select and purchase clothing and accessories for retail stores or fashion brands, considering market trends and consumer preferences.

    • Fashion Entrepreneur:

      • Start and manage your own fashion-related business, such as a boutique, brand, or design studio.

    • Fashion Educator:

      • Teach fashion design and related subjects at educational institutions or through workshops and seminars.
