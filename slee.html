State level Entrance Exams

Let's Make A Correct Choice

Overview

Purpose:

Entrance exams for admission into technical and professional courses at the state level.

Scope:

Varies from state to state, targeting institutions within the respective state.

Organizer:

Respective State Governments or State Universities.

Subjects Tested:

Typically include Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics for engineering. Medical and other courses may include Biology.

Eligibility:

Can include pharmacy, architecture, medical, and other allied health sciences.

Examples of State-Level Exams:

Maharashtra:

MHT-CET (Maharashtra Health and Technical Common Entrance Test).

Andhra Pradesh & Telangana:

EAMCET (Engineering, Agriculture and Medical Common Entrance Test).

Karnataka:

KCET (Karnataka Common Entrance Test).

West Bengal:

WBJEE (West Bengal Joint Entrance Examination).

Admission Process:

Counseling and Seat Allocation: Based on the rank obtained in the respective state-level exam.

Reservation Policies:

Vary as per the state government's norms.

(Frequency)Annual Exams:

Generally conducted once a year.
