    • Careers

    • Research Scientist:

      • Conduct original research to expand scientific knowledge in disciplines such as physics, chemistry, biology, or mathematics.

    • University Professor:

      • Teach and mentor students at the university level, while conducting research in your chosen field of pure sciences.

    • Science Writer/Journalist:

      • Communicate scientific concepts to the public through writing, journalism, or media, making science accessible to a broader audience.

    • Science Policy Analyst:

      • Evaluate and analyze scientific policies, providing insights and recommendations to government agencies or organizations.

    • Data Scientist:

      • Apply statistical and computational techniques to analyze and interpret complex data sets in various scientific domains.

    • Environmental Scientist:

      • Study the environment and work towards solutions for environmental challenges, such as climate change, pollution, and conservation.

    • Biotechnologist:

      • Apply biological knowledge to develop technologies and products in fields such as medicine, agriculture, and industry.

    • Mathematician:

      • Explore mathematical theories, develop models, and apply mathematical principles to solve real-world problems.

    • Science Educator:

      • Teach science at the school or college level, inspiring and educating the next generation of scientists.

    • Science Communicator:

      • Engage in public outreach, explaining scientific concepts through various mediums such as exhibits, presentations, or podcasts.
