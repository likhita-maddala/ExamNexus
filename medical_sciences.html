    • Careers

    • Medical Doctor (Physician):

      • Diagnose, treat, and prevent illnesses and injuries, providing comprehensive medical care to patients.

    • Nurse:

      • Provide patient care, administer medications, and assist with medical procedures under the supervision of physicians.

    • Pharmacist:

      • Dispense medications, provide drug information, and ensure the safe use of pharmaceuticals.

    • Medical Researcher:

      • Conduct scientific research to advance medical knowledge and contribute to the development of new treatments and therapies.

    • Medical Laboratory Scientist:

      • Perform laboratory tests to analyze body fluids, tissues, and cells, aiding in the diagnosis and treatment of diseases.

    • Physical Therapist:

      • Assist patients in recovering from injuries or surgeries, focusing on physical rehabilitation and mobility.

    • Public Health Specialist:

      • Promote and implement public health programs, policies, and interventions to improve community well-being.

    • Health Educator:

      • Educate individuals and communities on health-related topics, disease prevention, and healthy lifestyle choices.

    • Medical Illustrator:

      • Create visual representations of medical concepts, procedures, and anatomy for educational and instructional purposes.

    • Healthcare Administrator:

      • Manage and oversee the operations of healthcare facilities, ensuring efficient and effective delivery of medical services.
