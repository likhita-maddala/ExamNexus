    • Careers

    • Registered Nurse:

      • Provide patient care, administer medications, and collaborate with healthcare teams to promote well-being.

    • Physical Therapist:

      • Assist patients in regaining mobility and managing pain through physical rehabilitation techniques.

    • Occupational Therapist:

      • Help individuals develop or regain skills for daily living and work after illness or injury.

    • Speech-Language Pathologist:

      • Assess and treat communication and swallowing disorders, working with individuals of all ages.

    • Respiratory Therapist:

      • Assist patients with respiratory issues, providing treatment and education on breathing techniques and equipment.

    • Medical Laboratory Technologist:

      • Perform laboratory tests to analyze body fluids, tissues, and cells, aiding in the diagnosis and treatment of diseases.

    • Radiologic Technologist:

      • Operate imaging equipment to perform diagnostic imaging exams, such as X-rays and CT scans.

    • Dietitian/Nutritionist:

      • Assess and provide nutritional guidance to individuals and communities for maintaining health and managing conditions.

    • Medical Social Worker:

      • Provide support and counseling to patients and their families, addressing social and emotional aspects of healthcare.

    • Health Educator:

      • Educate individuals and communities on health-related topics, disease prevention, and healthy lifestyle choices.
